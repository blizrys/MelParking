The Melbourne City Parking Insight

Our goal is to find an insight that could potentially given to VICRoad’s Executives for city development plan on what is missing and how could they taken next step to handle better parking spaces in The Melbourne City.

Based on www.melbourne.vic.gov.au website, The city of Melbourne municipality includes 14 suburbs with the size approximate 37.7 km2.

However, Melbourne Open Data (data.melbourne.vic.gov.au) has subdivide The city of Melbourne into 17 postcode areas. This show inconsistent of data even from the goverment websites.

Insight #1

The information given from Melbourne Goverment websites shows that even the official known data source cannot clearly defined the areas. Although, this might not cause any problem becuase postal address use postcode to locate actual locations. However, due to poorly defined on the area and suburb name. This could cause some confusion when need to verbally communicate. It it recommend that Goverment should take this into consideration and improve this matter

To make our understanding simple and consistence across this report, we will use 12 suburbs. This was conclude by using data from www.melbourne.vic.gov.au which has 14 suburbs and group 'South Wharf-South Bank' and 'Kensington-Flemington' together as the areas are not clearly merged between 2 data sources.

Parking spaces

The city of Melbourne is a major city that people traverse both for work and pleasure. The chart on the right show an overview of available parking space within the city. Totally, there are 1.2M Parking spaces available.

Our initial observation show that majorly parking space are offer as commercial off-streets. Only 2% (24k) of overall parking spaces are on-street parking which is very limited. This show that the city encourage travelers to use public transport or parking off-street.

Moreover, as the city are pushing on-street parking sensor to become smart city, only 20% (5k) of on-street parking spaces has been implemented.

Hover to see actual number count

Since we only have data from on-street sensor, we our further report will only be based on these collected information. In other words, we will focus on available data from 5k on-street parking sensor out of 24K on-street parking spaces.

On average, Carton South is the usual area that traveller parked thier car desprite of time of the day. This goes with respect available on-street parking spaces offer in that area. Moreover if we look closely, Carton South usually be crowded all day especially during afternoon and evening time. While other parking area only crowded during afternoon.

Looking from the graph, we might conclude that dockland would be the best parking area to traverse to the city.

Insight #2

The interesting knowledge we learned from our data is that most traveller choose to park in the northern area of the city or Melbourne. This might lead to question back to the Government whether this are their intention or not? If not, does melbourne has enough parking space offer for travellers that come from the south side of the city?

However, our data exclude area of 'South Melbourne'. We suggest that Government should include and setup sensor onto this area. Given if we have more information on this, could lead to proper insight and planning to accommondate parking spaces for travellers into the city.

Insight #3

On average, drivers got fined for parking overtime about 13k(?) per month. We could see that the ratio of parking fined record in each area actually goes propotional to number of parking sensors. Having fined can either mean people are not following the rules or rules might not suitable for people behavior. But because this happen at the same ratio through out the year. Goverment should be considering adjusting the parking restriction. This shows that parking space inside the city has parking restriction time that might not be making sense to how driver needed for parking.

Alternatively, from the start of our insight towards the data. Lots of Off-street parking space is available. Why does people chose to parking on-street rather than off-street. Our first assumption would be the price they charged for parking. Goverment should also take a look into the overall prices parking space. if it does make sense for people that require to travel to city frequently.

Conclusion

Our initial observation towards parking space give us some interesting insights. The first interesting point is that off-street parking space have more than on streets. The second interesting insight is that there are only limited number of sensor setup so far. The third observation is that Carlton South is the most crowded area (and also most parking space). The forth observation parking fined might not be make sense.
